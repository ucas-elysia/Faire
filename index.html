Abstract

Solving complex geometric problems inherently requires interleaved reasoning: a tight alternation between constructing diagrams and performing logical deductions. Although recent MLLMs show strong capabilities in visual generation and plotting, we identify a counterintuitive and underexplored phenomenon.

Naively applying Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) on interleaved plot–solution data leads to a substantial degradation in reasoning performance compared to text-only baselines. We argue that this failure stems from a fundamental limitation of SFT, which primarily induces distributional alignment: the model learns to reproduce the surface format of interleaved plotting but fails to internalize the causal dependency between the generated plot and reasoning steps.

To overcome this limitation, we propose Faire (Functional alignment for interleaved reasoning), a reinforcement-learning framework that enforces three causal constraints to move beyond superficial imitation toward functional alignment. Extensive experiments show that Faire induces a qualitative shift in model behavior in which the plotting is effectively internalized, yielding competitive performance on challenging geometric reasoning benchmarks.

Key Claims

  1. ① The SFT Paradox. Mimicking the format of interleaved reasoning without its function hurts accuracy. SFT acts as a noisy channel — the diagram raises the entropy of the answer rather than collapsing it.
  2. ② Functional Alignment via Tri-Perspective Verification. A gated reward over geometric, perceptual, and semantic validity forces the diagram to behave as a causal mediator T → V → Y.
  3. ③ The Aha Moment is Measurable. Under RL, entropy reallocates onto visual & plotting tokens (23.4%) — drawing becomes an active decision shaped by verifier feedback, not memorized formatting.

The SFT Paradox — why drawing usually hurts

Existing wisdom says richer multimodal context should improve reasoning. We find the opposite on geometry: when we SFT on the same data with diagrams interleaved, accuracy drops below text-only. The model has learned when to draw, but not what to draw such that drawing helps.

SFT — distributional alignment
  • Superficial correlation. Minimizes $H_\pi(V \mid T, X)$, treats $V$ and $Y$ as conditionally independent given strong textual priors.
  • Context fragmentation. A mismatched diagram acts as a noisy channel; the deduction entropy rises: $H_{\pi_\theta}(Y \mid T_1, V, X) \geq H_{\pi_\theta}(Y \mid T_1, X) + \delta$.
  • Drawing as decoration. The figure looks plausible but does not faithfully implement the claimed visual hierarchy.
Faire — functional alignment
  • $V$ as latent causal mediator. RL optimizes $T \xrightarrow{\text{construct}} V \xrightarrow{\text{observe}} Y$ with verified mediation.
  • Tri-perspective reward. Joint constraints on geometric, perceptual, and semantic validity collapse, not raise, the answer entropy.
  • Drawing as evidence. The constructed diagram is operationally consistent with the proof — auxiliary points instantiated in the right dependency order.

Faire Framework

Faire treats the visual artifact $V$ as a latent causal mediator between the textual premise $T$ and the answer $Y$. For the causal path $T \to V \to Y$ to hold, $V$ must satisfy three independent conditions, formalized by a Tri-perspective Verifier system $\mathcal{V}$.

🧮

$\mathcal{C}_{\text{geo}}$ — Node Integrity

An objective verifier executes the generated code in a GeoGebra sandbox and checks it against hard constraints derived from the problem (collinearity, midpoint, tangency …). Guarantees $V$ exists as a well-defined mathematical object.

$$\mathcal{C}_{\text{geo}}(c, X) = \mathbb{1}(c \in \Omega_{\text{valid}}) \cdot \!\!\prod_{k \in \mathcal{K}_X}\!\! \mathbb{1}(\text{Satisfies}(c, k))$$
👁️

$\mathcal{C}_{\text{perc}}$ — Egress Validity

A subjective verifier (an MLLM judge) checks that $V$ is actually readable by a solver — occlusion, extreme scale, and label overlap act as channel noise that breaks the $V \to Y$ edge.

$$\mathcal{C}_{\text{perc}}(v) = \mathbb{1}\bigl(J_{\text{mllm}}(v) > \tau_{\text{perc}}\bigr)$$
🔗

$\mathcal{C}_{\text{sem}}$ — Ingress Validity

A semantic verifier requires logical entailment between primitives extracted from the code and intents extracted from the text — no random "valid figure that ignores the plan".

$$\mathcal{C}_{\text{sem}}(c, T) = \mathbb{1}\bigl(\psi(T) \subseteq \varphi(c)\bigr)$$
Causally-grounded reward
$$R(V, Y) \;=\; \mathbb{1}(Y = Y^{*}) \;+\; \beta \cdot \bigl(\mathcal{C}_{\text{geo}} + \mathcal{C}_{\text{perc}} + \mathcal{C}_{\text{sem}}\bigr)$$
GRPO objective
$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{GRPO}} \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{\tau \sim \pi_\theta}\!\left[\frac{\pi_\theta(\tau)}{\pi_{\text{old}}(\tau)}\,\hat{A}(\tau)\right] \;-\; \beta\, D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(\pi_\theta \,\|\, \pi_{\text{ref}}\bigr)$$

The functionally-aligned reward mines the long-tail of the generation distribution, reinforcing those rare outlier trajectories where the diagram actively facilitates deduction — not just decorates the answer.

Are the verifier signals trustworthy?

Faire-Bench's filtering and reward design lean on automated verifiers — so they had better track human judgment. We run 12 MLLMs through both the tri-perspective verifier and a panel of human raters; the agreement is near-perfect.

Main Results

Faire (built on Qwen3-VL-8B, 8B params) reaches 74.82% accuracy and 45.37 verification average — outperforming proprietary baselines including Gemini-2.5-Pro and GPT-5.2 by a wide margin on functional grounding, while remaining competitive on raw answer accuracy.

Accuracy is not enough.

Gemini-2.5-Pro hits the highest answer accuracy, yet its formal validity stays at 22.51. It reasons around the diagram, not through it. Faire pairs strong accuracy with nearly 3× the formal grounding (60.39 vs 22.51).

Surface mimicry vs. causal grounding.

GPT-5.2 produces diagrams with high SSIM/LPIPS — visually plausible — but its Parser score collapses to 7.02. Faire avoids this pitfall by prioritizing verifiable relations over surface resemblance.

RL > Best-of-N selection.

Best-of-N on the SFT checkpoint with the same verifiers reaches only 37.92 Draw Avg even at N=10 — Faire's 45.37 can't be reached by selecting better SFT traces; it requires repairing the construction–reasoning coupling.

✦ The Aha Moment — entropy migrates onto drawing

Aha here is not a vibe — it is a measurable shift in the entropy distribution. Comparing the top-100 most entropy-increased tokens between SFT and RL: nearly 40% of the shift lands on numeric and code tokens, with Visual & Plotting tokens taking the single largest share at 23.38%.
